Monday, 24 October

Day 2 - Paris

Route
Mercure Paris Roissy CDGRoissy-en-France→Eiffel TowerChamp de Mars→La Fontaine de JadeRue du Bosquet→Louvre MuseumRue de Rivoli→Galeries Lafayette HaussmannBoulevard Haussmann→The TerraceGaleries Lafayette→Place Edouard VIIParis→Bateaux MouchesSeine River Cruise

Visited a very crowded Eiffel Tower in the morning, and had a good Chinese Zi-Char style lunch. Stopped by the Louvre Museum for some photos, then headed to Galeries Lafayette for some shopping and Cafe Food & Ice Cream, with a nice top floor view at The Terrace. Dinner was quite underwhelming with a bland tasting Steak, and I tried Escargots for the first time. Not a fan. Ended the day a night Seine River Cruise, but would have preferred to go for the cruise in the day as everything looked similar at night.

Day 4 - The Netherlands

Route

One of my favourite days of the Trip. Started off the day heading to the Windmill Village, where we saw a few demonstrations such as the Wooden Shoe Carving and the Cheese Factory. Later in the afternoon, we headed to a Fishing Village at Volendam for lunch. Another underwhelming meal with an overcooked steak :( Thereafter, we headed to Amsterdam for another River Cruise. At this point River Cruises were kind of boring so I switched to Japanese Audio for the Audio Tour to practice my Japanese. We also headed to Red Light District but no photos were allowed.

Friday, 28 October

Day 6 - Heidelberg, the Black Forest & Rhine Falls

Route
NH Mannheim ViernheimGermany→Heidelberg Old BridgeHeidelberg→Heidelberg CastleHeidelberg→Black ForestBaden-Wurttemberg→Lake TitiseeTitisee-Neustadt→Cuckoo Clock WorkshopBlack Forest→Rhine FallsSchaffhausen→Movenpick Hotel EgerkingenEgerkingen

Started off with a very cool day and visited Heidelberg Castle. Later, we drove past Germany's Black Forest and had some German pork knuckle for lunch. Also stopped by Lake Titisee for some photos, and we learned about the history of Cuckoo Clocks. We then drove to Switzerland's Rhine Falls and had some really delicious Chicken Tikka Masala that cost 18 Swiss Francs. So happy that I ate it because there was a traffic jam due to an accident and the bus driver also made a wrong turn which caused us to reach our hotel almost 3 hours later at around 9pm.

Saturday, 29 October

Day 7 - Mount Titlis & Lucerne

Route
Movenpick Hotel EgerkingenEgerkingen→EngelbergObwalden→Titlis RotairMount Titlis→Glacier CaveMount Titlis→Ice FlyerMount Titlis→Cliff WalkMount Titlis→Panorama RestaurantMount Titlis→Lion MonumentLucerne→Reuss River & Needle DamLucerne→Bachmann BakeryLucerne

One of the best days of the trip. Headed to an excursion at Mount Titlis where we took a cable car to the midway point then took the world's first Revolving Cable Car. There were quite a few activities at the mountain such as the Glacier Cave, Cliff Walk and the Ski Lift. Had a great lunch at the Panorama Restaurant, then we headed to Lucerne for some sightseeing. Ended the day with one of the most value for money meals - Pizza Poulet Curry which cost 13 Swiss Francs that the two of us shared and struggled to finish.

Sunday, 30 October – Tuesday, 1 November

Day 8 - Colmar & Zurich

Route

Final day of the trip! Time passed so quickly. Started off the day heading back to France, Colmar. Took a tram ride in the morning for a tour of Colmar. Visited Little Venice and had one of the best meals - Steak. We walked through the city and saw some churches. Thereafter, we headed to Zürich and saw a nice Statue. One of the coolest things we saw was people playing Outdoor Giant Chess. With that, we made our way to Zürich airport for our flight back.
